1. Causes of Metabolic Changes
Belly Fat & Metabolism
Q: Why am I gaining belly fat during menopause even if I eat the same?
A: Estrogen decline shifts fat storage to the abdomen, lowers muscle mass, and worsens insulin resistance.
Insulin Resistance & Visceral Fat
Q: How does menopause affect metabolism?
A: Metabolism slows by about 100¢200 calories daily due to muscle loss and hormonal shifts, making weight harder to manage.
Muscle Loss (Sarcopenia)
Q: Why does muscle loss matter?
A: Less muscle means slower fat burning, as muscle tissue burns more calories than fat.
Hormonal Stress & Cortisol
Q: How does stress cause weight gain?
A: Hormone fluctuations raise cortisol, which promotes abdominal fat and insulin resistance. Chronic stress worsens this.
Other Contributors
Q: What else contributes?
A: Thyroid dips, poor sleep, and gut microbiome changes from estrogen loss can all slow metabolism.

2. Solutions for Managing Weight & Metabolism
Nutrition Strategies
Q: What diet works best?
A: Anti-inflammatory, high-protein, high-fiber meals with healthy fats. About 100¢150g protein daily, 25¢35g fiber, and healthy fats like avocado and olive oil.
Q: Does intermittent fasting help?
A: Yes, a 12¢14h overnight fast can improve insulin sensitivity and fat burning. Stop if it causes fatigue or stress.
Q: Should I track macros?
A: Yes, at first. A common balance is 40% protein, 30% fat, 30% carbs.
Q: Can supplements help?
A: Creatine supports muscle, omega-3s reduce inflammation, and magnesium helps recovery. Always check with your doctor.

Exercise Approaches
Q: What exercise is best for metabolism?
A: Strength training 2¢3x/week plus HIIT 1¢2x/week. Aim for 150 minutes total activity weekly.
Q: Why is strength training important?
A: Lifting weights builds muscle, increases metabolism, and burns fat even without calorie restriction.
Q: How does HIIT help?
A: Short bursts of intense effort reduce belly fat and improve insulin sensitivity without overloading cortisol.
Q: Should I avoid long cardio?
A: Yes. Long steady-state cardio may raise cortisol and promote fat storage.

Stress & Recovery
Q: How does sleep affect metabolism?
A: Poor sleep disrupts hormones and increases cravings. 7¢9 hours improves insulin sensitivity.
Q: How can I lower cortisol?
A: Prioritize sleep, limit caffeine, reduce alcohol, and practice yoga or mindfulness.
Q: WhatÆs the ōpower windowö approach?
A: Train hard but recover fully. This lowers cortisol and supports long-term fat loss.

Hormone Therapy (HT)
Q: Can hormone therapy help with weight?
A: HT can improve fat distribution and insulin sensitivity but isnÆt a weight loss treatment. It supports overall metabolic balance.
