? Nutrition in Menopause Ś Knowledge Base

1. Anti-Inflammatory Diet
Q: What is an anti-inflammatory diet?
A: A way of eating that focuses on whole foods like fish, nuts, seeds, leafy greens, berries, colorful veggies, and healthy fats while limiting sugar, fried, and processed foods.
Q: Why is this important in menopause?
A: Declining estrogen increases inflammation, which worsens hot flashes, fatigue, mood swings, and joint pain. This diet calms inflammation and protects long-term health.

2. High Protein Intake
Q: How much protein do I need?
A: Aim for 1¢1.5 grams of protein per pound of ideal body weight daily, split across meals.
Q: Why does protein matter in menopause?
A: It prevents muscle loss, boosts metabolism, reduces overeating, and stabilizes blood sugar.

3. Fiber Consumption
Q: How much fiber should I eat?
A: Around 25¢35g daily from veggies, fruits, grains, nuts, seeds, and legumes.
Q: Why is fiber helpful in menopause?
A: Fiber improves digestion, balances blood sugar, supports gut health, reduces inflammation, and lowers cholesterol.

4. Intermittent Fasting
Q: What is intermittent fasting?
A: Eating in a time window, like 16:8 (e.g., eat between 10 a.m. and 6 p.m.), while focusing on nutrient-dense meals.
Q: Why can fasting help in menopause?
A: It improves insulin control, supports fat burning (especially belly fat), reduces inflammation, and helps with weight management.

5. Macronutrient Balance (Fuel Refocus)
Q: How should I balance carbs, fats, and protein?
A: Try around 40¢50% healthy fats, 25¢30% protein, and 20¢30% complex carbs.
Q: Why adjust macros in menopause?
A: It helps your body burn fat for energy, stabilize blood sugar, and reduce cravings.

6. Targeted Supplements
Q: Do I need supplements in menopause?
A: Food comes first, but supplements can fill gaps. Common ones include omega-3s, vitamin D + K2, creatine, magnesium, collagen, and probiotics.
Q: Why?
A: Nutrient needs rise in menopause. Supplements support bones, muscles, mood, sleep, and overall health.
