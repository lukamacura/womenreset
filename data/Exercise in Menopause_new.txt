1. Strength Training (Resistance Exercise)
Q: What is strength training?
A: ItÆs exercise using weights or your own bodyweight to strengthen major muscles. Examples: squats, lunges, deadlifts, push-ups.
Q: How often should I do it?
A: Aim for 2¢3 sessions per week, 30¢45 minutes each.
Q: Why is it important in menopause?
A: Estrogen decline causes muscle loss and bone weakness. Strength training builds lean muscle, strengthens bones, and reduces fall risk.

2. Weight-Bearing & Impact Activities
Q: What are weight-bearing and impact exercises?
A: Activities like brisk walking, hiking, dancing, jogging, step-ups, or heel drops that use your body against gravity.
Q: How often should I do them?
A: 3¢5 times per week for 30¢60 minutes. Add short impact sessions (10¢15 min) 2¢3 times weekly.
Q: Why are they helpful?
A: They keep bones strong, especially hips and spine, reducing osteoporosis and fracture risk.

3. High-Intensity Interval Training (HIIT)
Q: What is HIIT?
A: Short bursts of intense cardio (like sprints or cycling) followed by recovery.
Q: How often should I do HIIT?
A: 1¢2 sessions per week, 20¢30 minutes total including warm-up/cool-down.
Q: Why is HIIT good for menopause?
A: It improves heart health, burns fat (especially belly fat), and boosts mood and energy.

4. Mobility, Balance & Core Training
Q: What counts as mobility and balance training?
A: Yoga, Pilates, tai chi, planks, single-leg stands, and stretches.
Q: How often should I do it?
A: 1¢2 sessions per week, or 5¢10 minutes added into other workouts.
Q: Why is this important in menopause?
A: It improves posture, reduces fall risk, strengthens the core, lowers stress, and improves sleep.

5. Additional Tips
Q: How should I recover after exercise?
A: Prioritize rest days, sleep 7¢8 hours, and avoid overtraining.
Q: How much protein do I need?
A: Around 1.2¢2.0 g per kg of body weight daily to support muscle repair.
Q: Do I need to adapt exercise to my symptoms?
A: Yes. Start where you are, progress gradually, and adjust if symptoms like joint pain or fatigue flare up.
Q: WhatÆs a good weekly plan?
A: 2 strength sessions, 1¢2 HIIT, 2¢3 weight-bearing, and 1¢2 mobility sessions. Aim for 150¢300 minutes of activity per week.
Q: Is mindset important?
A: Absolutely. Exercise builds confidence and helps you feel in control during a time of change.
