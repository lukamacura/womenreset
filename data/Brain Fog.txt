1. Symptoms & Causes
Q: What is brain fog in menopause?
A: Brain fog feels like mental fuzziness, memory lapses, trouble finding words, and difficulty concentrating.
Q: Why does brain fog happen?
A: Estrogen decline slows brain energy and neuron activity. Poor sleep, hot flashes, and stress make it worse.

2. Lifestyle Solutions
Q: What habits help brain clarity?
A: Prioritize good sleep, manage stress, and follow a consistent routine. Mindfulness, yoga, and relaxation support focus.
Q: Can exercise help?
A: Yes. Cardio (30 min, 3¢5x/week) and strength training boost blood flow and BDNF, improving memory and focus by up to 40%.

3. Brain Training
Q: How can I keep my brain sharp?
A: Stimulate it daily with puzzles, reading, or learning new skills. This builds cognitive reserve.

4. Nutrition & Supplements
Q: What foods support brain health?
A: A Mediterranean diet with leafy greens, berries, nuts, and fatty fish. Omega-3s (1¢2g EPA/DHA daily) may support memory.
Q: Are supplements useful?
A: Omega-3s, antioxidants, magnesium, and B vitamins may help. Always check with your doctor before starting supplements.

5. Monitoring & Therapy
Q: When should I see a doctor?
A: If brain fog is severe or persistent. Doctors can check thyroid, vitamin levels, or do cognitive tests.
Q: Can HRT help?
A: Early hormone therapy may stabilize brain energy for some women.
